Add to Calendar

How to Get Started

Thank you for installing Add to Calendar. Here's how you can start using it:

  1. Highlight any date or time text on a webpage.
  2. Right-click and select Add to Calendar from the context menu.
  3. A new Google Calendar event will open with the selected date and time pre-filled.

Zoom Meeting Integration

Our latest feature lets you easily add Zoom meeting details (topic & link) to your Google Calendar event. Simply highlight any snippet with a Zoom link and/or "Topic" line, right-click, and select "Add to Calendar."

Here are three common examples you might see in an email or online:

Format Example Snippet Outcome in Google Calendar
Format 1 
Topic: Sales Lead With John Doe
Join Zoom Meeting
https://us06web.zoom.us/j/xxxxxx?pwd=595969595994.1

Meeting ID: 000 00000 000
Passcode: 00000
  • Title: Sales Lead With John Doe
  • Location: Zoom link automatically added
  • Time: If a valid date/time is highlighted, it will be parsed and added.
Format 2 
Topic: AI Tutor
Time: Jan 13, 2025 01:00 PM London

Join Zoom Meeting
https://us06web.zoom.us/j/xxxxxx?pwd=595969595994.1
  • Title: AI Tutor
  • Location: Zoom link automatically added
  • Time: "Jan 13, 2025 01:00 PM London" is recognized and parsed accordingly.
Format 3 
Time: Jan 13, 2025 01:00 PM London

https://us06web.zoom.us/j/xxxxxx?pwd=595969595994.1
  • Title: Defaults to "New Event" (since "Topic" is missing)
  • Location: Zoom link automatically added
  • Time: "Jan 13, 2025 01:00 PM London" is parsed into the event details.

Tip: If the snippet includes a "Topic" line, that will be used as your Google Calendar event’s title. Otherwise, "New Event" is used as a fallback. If a valid Zoom link (zoom.us/j/... ) is detected, it will appear in the "Location" field of your event.

Multi-Region Time Zone Support

We now recognize common time zone abbreviations or region names in your highlighted text. For instance:

  • EST, CST, PST, WAT, GMT
  • London, Lagos, Berlin, New York
  • UTC+1, UTC-5 (and more)

If we detect a phrase like Jan 13, 2025 01:00 PM WAT or Jan 13, 2025 01:00 PM London, your calendar event will automatically convert and reflect the correct time based on that zone. We default to your system time zone if none is found.

Examples:

Snippet Detected Time Zone Event Time in GCal
Jan 13, 2025 2:00 PM PST Pacific Standard Time (America/Los_Angeles) Parsed as Jan 13, 2025, 14:00 PST (UTC-8), then converted to the final event with ctz=America/Los_Angeles
Jan 13, 2025 01:00 PM WAT West Africa Time (Africa/Lagos) Parsed as Jan 13, 2025, 13:00 WAT (UTC+1), then ctz=Africa/Lagos in Google Calendar

This means you'll see the correct local time on your Google Calendar, even if the event was scheduled in a different region. No extra setup required!
